Brain Computer Interface

People can control a robotic arm with only their minds

University of Minnesota have created history! They have developed a robotic arm that can be controlled by the human mind. They hope the new development will help those who are paralyzed or have trouble with their arms. To make the robot arm move, the controller only has to imagine moving their arms. The most critical piece of the project is the electroencephalography (EEG) cap. It records weak electrical activity of the controllers brain and convert the "thoughts" to reality through the robotic arm. To test the technology, the school picked 8 healthy random subjects to complete the experimental checkpoints. All eight were able to control the arm. According to researcher Bin He, the next step is to examine how examine how this technology could work with someone who has had a stroke or is paralyzed. 3D Printing

Scientists build new ultrasound device using 3D printing technology

Scientists from Nanyang Technological University, Singapore have created a ultrasound device that create images through 3D printed lenses. The device will allow doctors to have better control and precision when operating. They hope it will be very helpful in terms of killing tumours, lossen blood clots, and deliver drugs into targeted cells. The lenses the device uses are 3D printed and therefore cheaper and easier to produce. Most ultrasounds are limited to spherical shapes, however the 3D printed lenses can allow more complex shapes.

As a result, images are sharper. The 3D printed lenses allow ultrasound waves to be at multiple sites or shape the focus specially to a target.

The new ultrasound device overcomes the limits of glass. It opens new doors for research and medical surgey. It also will promote new medical techniques and research opportunities. It will really be helpful when it comes to cancer research and procedures. With the new technology, doctors will be able to tell the difference of cancerous cells and non-cancerous cells. Cloud Computing

Google, a Cloud-Computing Upstart, Seeks Credibility

Google conquered internet search. It is having a tougher time winning the rapidly growing cloud-computing industry. Google must build more credibility among corporations wary of running their businesses on the servers of an internet-search company with little experience supporting the technology needs of big companies, other than its own. The shift to cloud computing threatens to displace billions of dollars of tech spending as companies from corporate giants to hot startups store their data and run their digital businesses on other companies' computer servers.The company's cloud business had been geared toward app developers and tech firms, and some of its biggest clients are still Snap Inc. and Spotify AB.Google is now bolstering its services for traditional companies by hiring workers specialized in industries such as health care, finance and retail, to help make Google's cloud products work for those sectors. Google said it added more cloud employees than any other division last year, and is in the process of hiring 1,000 employees focused on cloud customers.
